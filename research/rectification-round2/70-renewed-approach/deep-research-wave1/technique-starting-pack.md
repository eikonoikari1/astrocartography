# Technique Starting Pack

## Purpose

Define the first techniques to use in the full-day rectification experiments.

The rule is simple:
- start with a small pack
- measure each technique separately
- only bundle after single-technique behavior is known

Do not start by throwing every doctrine into the model.

## Starting Pack Recommendation

### Tier 1: Core full-day pack

Start with these four technique families:
- transits
- solar arcs
- secondary progressions
- annual profections

This pack is the best balance of breadth, birth-time sensitivity, and interpretability for the first experiments.

### Tier 2: Conditional addition

Add solar returns only if:
- the return location is fixed and documented
- the corpus has enough location data to make the comparison fair
- the Tier 1 pack already shows real separability

### Tier 3: Late-stage stress tests

Keep these out of the first pass:
- primary directions
- zodiacal releasing
- firdaria

They are valuable, but they are not the best first diagnostic layer.

## Why This Pack

### Transits
Transits are the broad trigger layer.
They are the easiest to compute and the easiest to compare against exact dated events.
They also work as a sanity check: if transits cannot create useful structure on the corpus, the rest of the stack is unlikely to rescue it.

Important detail:
- score transits to planets, angles, and house cusps separately
- do not limit them to planet-planet aspects only

Angle and cusp hits are where birth-time sensitivity matters most.

### Solar Arcs
Solar arcs are a strong directional layer.
They are suitable for the first experiments because they are explicit, smooth, and tightly tied to natal geometry.
Astrodienst describes solar arc direction as moving all horoscope factors by the Sun's daily motion, which makes it a natural intermediate-resolution timing method. [Source](https://www.astro.com/astrowiki/en/Solar_Arc)

### Secondary Progressions
Secondary progressions are the standard developmental timing layer.
They are useful for life periods and long-term transitions, and Astrodienst explicitly notes that minute-accurate birth time is required for meaningful use of progressed axes. [Source](https://www.astro.com/astrowiki/en/Secondary_Progression)

### Annual Profections
Profections give yearly chapter structure.
They are valuable for the first pass because they are a clean bridge between broad life themes and year-level activation.
Astrodienst describes profections as a repeating twelve-year activation structure. [Source](https://www.astro.com/astrowiki/en/Profection)

## Why Not Start With The Exact-Sensitive Methods

### Primary Directions
Primary directions are extremely sensitive.
Astrodienst explicitly states that the method needs birth time accurate to within a minute and that a one-minute error can produce about a year of error. [Source](https://www.astro.com/astrowiki/en/Primary_Directions)

That makes primary directions excellent as a late-stage stress test.
It also makes them risky as a first-pass pack if the corpus or event windows are noisy.

### Zodiacal Releasing
Zodiacal releasing is powerful, but it requires stricter doctrinal discipline and exact birth-time handling.
It should not be the first thing you reach for when you are still asking whether the corpus shows any useful separability at all.

### Firdaria
Firdaria is useful context, but not the first truth test.

### Solar Returns
Solar returns are valuable, but they introduce a location-at-return choice.
That is an extra moving part.
Keep them as a conditional addition after the core pack has proven value.

## Suggested Experimental Order

1. Transits
2. Solar Arcs
3. Secondary Progressions
4. Annual Profections
5. Solar Returns, only on cases with stable location policy
6. Primary Directions as a late-stage confirmation and stress test
7. Zodiacal Releasing and Firdaria after the core engine is already working

This order is practical, not doctrinally pure.
It favors fast falsification.

## What Each Technique Should Score

### Transits
Use for:
- exact dated events
- crisis windows
- public milestones
- relocations
- relationship turning points

### Solar Arcs
Use for:
- major turning points
- broader event windows
- angular activation
- transitions that span months rather than days

### Secondary Progressions
Use for:
- development arcs
- psychological or life-phase shifts
- long transitions
- periods that are better described as chapters than events

### Annual Profections
Use for:
- year-level chapter activation
- topic switching
- long life themes
- the bridge between a biography and a yearly timing frame

### Solar Returns
Use for:
- year-tone
- milestone years
- annual overlays
- cross-checking a candidate time that already looks plausible

## How To Score Techniques In The First Sweep

Keep the score channels separate first.

For each technique:
1. generate its activation outputs for each candidate bin
2. compare those outputs to the evidence packet
3. compute a per-technique score surface
4. normalize within person
5. compare the surfaces across techniques

Do not collapse everything into one blended score immediately.
That hides which technique is helping.

## What The First Pack Should Reveal

The starting pack should tell you:
- which techniques produce sharp peaks
- which techniques only give broad shape
- which techniques collapse under date fuzzing
- which techniques depend too heavily on already-good birth times

If the pack does not reveal clear differences, the corpus or scoring model needs work before more methods are added.

## What To Record Per Technique

For each person and technique record:
- score surface over all candidate bins
- true-bin rank
- top-3 windows
- peak width
- mode count
- sensitivity to evidence precision
- sensitivity to provenance tier
- contribution to combined score

## What To Defer

Do not put these into the first experiment bundle:
- multimodal cues
- free-form interview answers
- pairwise narrative prompts
- doctrine fusion
- location techniques

They are downstream additions, not the first truth test.

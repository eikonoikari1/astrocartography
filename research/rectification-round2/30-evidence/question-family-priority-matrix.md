# Question Family Priority Matrix

This matrix ranks question families by expected information gain for rectification, not by how astrologically familiar they feel.

## Ranking Rule

Prioritize families that:
- split stable life structure rather than mood adjectives
- are easy to phrase neutrally
- can be validated against known-time charts
- are less vulnerable to Barnum-style endorsement
- remain useful before the chart is almost solved

Delay or drop families that:
- are too generic
- are highly stereotype-driven
- only help after the posterior is already narrow
- look useful mainly because the wording is flattering

## What This Means Operationally

The core question bank should start with:
- career axis
- relationship axis
- family duty
- mobility
- crisis pattern
- identity pressure

Those families give the best balance of discrimination and neutrality.

The next layer should be:
- work style
- emotional posture

Those are worth testing only after the core life-structure families have done most of the pruning.

Body/style and public biography should not drive the posterior early. They are corroboration families, not primary inference families.

Generic temperament items should not be in the main path. They are too easy to validate subjectively and too weakly tied to a clear chart distinction.

## Meta-Formats That Are Not Families

The following should be used across families, not treated as families themselves:
- forced-choice presentation
- pairwise chart comparisons
- contradiction probes
- "not sure" responses

They are delivery mechanisms for the family, not the family.

## Selection Guidance

Use the highest-ranked families first when:
- the user has few dated events
- the goal is to prune a 24-hour search space
- the triage score is medium or high

Use lower-ranked families only when:
- the candidate interval is already narrow
- the user is visibly documented
- the chart family needs corroboration rather than discovery

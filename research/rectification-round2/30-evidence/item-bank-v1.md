# Item Bank V1

This is a first-pass question bank. Each item family exists to split a specific chart distinction.

## Item Families

| Family | Chart distinction being tested | Example item |
|---|---|---|
| Career axis | public visibility vs private work | "Which fits better: your life is organized around being seen, or around doing important work quietly?" |
| Relationship axis | partnership centrality vs independence | "Which is more true: relationships have defined your path, or you have mostly moved on your own course?" |
| Family duty | early responsibility vs relatively free development | "Did you feel pulled into responsibility early, or did you get more room to unfold on your own?" |
| Mobility | settled continuity vs recurring moves / reinvention | "Was change of place or identity a recurring feature, or did you stay more anchored?" |
| Crisis pattern | sharp turning points vs gradual evolution | "Did your life change in a few abrupt chapters, or in a slower continuous way?" |
| Temperament | outward force vs inward complexity | "Do people notice your presence quickly, or do they notice your depth later?" |
| Body / style | angular / marked presence vs understated presentation | "Do you have a physically memorable presence, or a more blended one?" |
| Work style | authority / management vs craft / service | "Do you tend to lead, coordinate, or set direction, or do you prefer hands-on problem solving?" |
| Emotional posture | guarded / controlled vs open / expressive | "Which is truer: you keep your cards close, or your feelings show readily?" |
| Identity pressure | singular life mission vs multiple competing roles | "Has one theme dominated your identity, or have you had to juggle several equally strong roles?" |

## Validation Rules

Each item family should satisfy three checks:

1. It must discriminate at least two plausible chart families.
2. It must not rely on a single flattering trait.
3. It must admit a clear contradiction path.

## Item Types

### Absolute item
One statement, one response.

Use only when:
- the trait is concrete
- the wording is neutral
- the answer is likely to be stable

### Forced-choice item
Two plausible life-shapes, one answer.

Use when:
- both sides are believable
- the goal is discrimination rather than description

### Pairwise chart comparison
Show two candidate chart narratives and ask which fits better.

Use when:
- the posterior is already narrowed
- the system needs a sharper split than generic self-report can provide

### Contradiction probe
Ask what is least true or where the current fit fails.

Use when:
- the current candidate looks too smooth

## First Revision Targets

- reduce generic adjective items
- expand life-structure items
- add more contradiction probes
- add a "not sure" path where the user lacks evidence
- keep item wording neutral and non-leading

# Parans

## Technique Profile

- Practice role: advanced location refinement
- Doctrine: locational astrology
- Birth-time sensitivity: exact required
- Status: planned

## What Question This Answers

Where do two planets become angular together, creating a more compound locational signature?

## Use When

- the birth time is exact
- standard line work has already narrowed the field
- you need a more sophisticated explanation for why a place feels unusually concentrated

## Do Not Use When

- you need a first-pass relocation tool
- the birth time is approximate
- you are using parans as a replacement for natal and relocated structure

## How to Read It

1. Identify the paired planets and their angular combination.
2. Read the natal condition of both planets.
3. Judge whether the pairing intensifies support, stress, or mixed outcomes.
4. Use parans to refine a location call already supported elsewhere.

## What Not to Do

- Do not treat every paran as equally strong.
- Do not use them to override the main locational picture.
- Do not hide birth-time fragility when presenting them.

Planned. The research base is strong enough to justify future implementation, but the technique needs precise math and tight practitioner framing.

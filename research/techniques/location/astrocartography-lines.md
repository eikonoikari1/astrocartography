# Astrocartography Lines

## Technique Profile

- Practice role: core location method
- Doctrine: locational astrology
- Birth-time sensitivity: required
- Status: implemented

## What Question This Answers

Where does a planet become angular and therefore far more emphasized in lived experience?

## Use When

- you need a first-pass map of promising or demanding locations
- you want to compare broad line families before deep-diving cities
- you are trying to understand why a location feels different from the natal baseline

## Do Not Use When

- the birth time is weak
- you are treating planetary lines as good or bad without natal context
- you want one line alone to answer the full relocation question

## How to Read It

1. Identify which planet is angular and on which angle.
2. Check the natal condition of that planet.
3. Read the line in the domain of its angle: AC, DC, MC, or IC.
4. Compare multiple lines before making a recommendation.

## What Not to Do

- Do not rank lines without checking natal condition.
- Do not assume benefic planets will always feel easy.
- Do not confuse planetary angularity with relocated house emphasis.

## Current App Support

`node astro.mjs lines`
`node astro.mjs score`
`node astro.mjs score-cities`

The app computes MC, IC, AS, and DS line structures and uses them in city scoring.

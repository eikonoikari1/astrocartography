# Transits

## Technique Profile

- Practice role: core supporting timing layer
- Doctrine: modern / hybrid
- Birth-time sensitivity: helpful, stronger when angles are available
- Status: implemented

## What Question This Answers

What is currently being triggered, and when does it become visible?

## Use When

- you already know the natal topic
- you need dates, peaks, or trigger windows
- you want to see whether a currently active chapter is being activated externally

## Do Not Use When

- you have not established natal promise
- you want transits to do the work of time-lord methods
- you are trying to force a major prediction from a weak transit alone

## How to Read It

1. Start with the natal condition of the planet being hit.
2. Separate climate transits from weather transits.
3. Give strongest weight to outer planets and to hits on angles, rulers, and already-activated planets.
4. Use exact dates as timing markers, not as isolated meanings.

## What Not to Do

- Do not let transits outrank natal promise.
- Do not read every transit with equal weight.
- Do not treat a transit hit as important just because it is exact.

## Current App Support

`node astro.mjs transits`

The app supports exact aspect search with outer-planet filtering and date ranges. It is strongest as the trigger layer in a wider synthesis.

# Synthesis Rules

This page defines how techniques should be combined. The goal is disciplined judgment, not technique accumulation.

## Reading Hierarchy

```text
natal promise
  -> active chapter / yearly activation
    -> annual overlay
      -> trigger timing
        -> place-specific amplification
```

In practice:

1. Read the natal baseline first.
2. Identify the activated topic or ruler.
3. Narrow to the relevant year or period.
4. Use triggers only after the larger context is clear.
5. Apply location methods when the question involves place.

## Weighting Rules

| Layer | What it tells you |
|---|---|
| Natal condition | What the chart can carry at all |
| Time-lord methods | Which topics and planets are switched on |
| Solar return / annual overlay | How the current year is shaped |
| Transits | When the activated material is likely to show visibly |
| Location methods | Where the experience is emphasized or expressed |

## The Convergence Rule

Convergence matters. It does not create certainty by itself.

- When multiple independent techniques point to the same planet, house, or theme, the topic gains importance.
- Convergence is strongest when the techniques belong to compatible doctrinal lanes.
- Convergence should sharpen emphasis, not justify inflated prediction.

## Conflict Resolution

When techniques disagree:

1. Give priority to natal promise over transient activation.
2. Give priority to broader timing layers over narrow triggers.
3. Give priority to methods with stronger input quality.
4. Name the contradiction directly instead of flattening it.

Example:
- A benefic relocation line may look attractive.
- If the natal planet is heavily burdened, the line may still amplify stress through that planet's topics.
- If the year is not activating that planet, the move may still matter, but less dramatically than the map suggests.

## What Not to Do

- Do not let transits overrule the natal chart.
- Do not let one dramatic technique erase three quieter but stronger ones.
- Do not turn every reading into an all-techniques reading.
- Do not speak about location with certainty when the birth time is loose.
- Do not treat implementation status as interpretive authority.

## Best-Fit Stacks

### Natal consultation
- natal chart
- planet condition
- house rulership chains
- optional: lots or fixed stars if the chart justifies them

### Timing consultation
- natal chart
- profections or zodiacal releasing
- solar return
- transits
- optional: progressions

### Relocation consultation
- natal chart
- ACG lines
- relocated chart
- current timing layer
- optional: cyclocartography, parans, local space

## Professional Standard

The job is to identify the governing logic of the reading. If a technique cannot explain why it belongs in the current consultation, leave it out.

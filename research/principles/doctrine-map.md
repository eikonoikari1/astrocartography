# Doctrine Map

This app supports more than one astrological lane. The right question is not "which technique is strongest?" The right question is "which school answers this question cleanly?"

## Doctrine Lanes

| Lane | Typical questions | Techniques | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Modern / hybrid natal | "What is this person like?" "What themes define the life?" | natal assessment, transits, progressions, solar returns | Good for descriptive and developmental work |
| Traditional / Hellenistic timing | "Which life chapter is active?" "Which topic is switched on this year?" | profections, lots, zodiacal releasing, firdaria | Strong on sequencing and activation |
| Locational astrology | "Where does this topic come alive?" | ACG lines, relocated charts, cyclocartography, parans, local space | Best when birth time is trustworthy |
| Horary | "What is the answer to this specific question asked now?" | horary only | Separate discipline with separate rules |

## What Not to Mix Casually

- Do not mix horary judgments into natal or relocation synthesis as if they are just another vote.
- Do not use sign-based time-lord systems without stating the house doctrine behind them.
- Do not combine modern outer-planet psychological framing with traditional timing systems without naming the blend explicitly.
- Do not use angle-dependent location techniques when birth time is weak and then speak with full confidence.

## Default Doctrine Policy for This Repo

Use an explicit hybrid stance unless a file says otherwise:

- Natal delineation may use modern planets and contemporary interpretive language.
- Timing methods should preserve their native traditional logic.
- Location work should stay grounded in astronomical and angular technique first, interpretation second.
- Horary should be labeled as a separate mode of practice.

## Professional Warning

Methodological clarity matters more than maximal technique count. A smaller, cleaner synthesis beats a stack of methods pulled from incompatible schools and read as if they all mean the same thing.
